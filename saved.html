The Movement of Water -- The Essence of Our Practice

Ever since moving to the Hudson Valley in 1987, the great Hudson River has been a source of inspiration and spiritual sustenance for me. Its waters are always moving, always changing, whether calm or stormy, white capped or ice encrusted. They remind me of a pearl of wisdom shared with me by Xerxes, a wise orthopedics resident, early in my family medicine training. He was a Zoroastrian-- an ancient religion originating in Persia, whose devotees believe that everything created is pure and should be treated with respect, including our natural world. In teaching me how to do an orthopedic evaluation on a patient, he said,
“remember Ron, where there's movement there's life.”
No statement better reflects the essence of Craniosacral Therapy. At the GRCTI we continually refine our abilities to listen to and follow the wavelike motion of the cerebrospinal fluid as it reverberates throughout the body. We learn to help melt and relieve obstructions to this flow. We support people of all backgrounds as we move through pain and trauma, and we celebrate together in the joy and laughter that are our birthrights. Come join us!

Sensing the wave motion of the craniosacral rhythm is true entry into the ultimate flow state.
